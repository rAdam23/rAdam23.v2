3. The Hypothesis

The Problem

RMIT Online provides prospective students with the ability to attend the universities higher education courses via its online portal.

It utilises an online student login platform 'Canvas' to provide students with access to the uni's 'Collaborate Ultra' workspace.

During my use of the platform it was clear that students had difficulty in locating webinars (subject lectures) due to unconventional naming and layouts within the workspace.

After confirming this hypothesis with desk research, I pursued this as a case study with the aim to improve the user experience design of the workspace.

4. Research

User Testing

Research consisted of developing a lean persona identifying the typical users of the workspace.

Once completed 9 individuals that matched the lean persona were interviewed for 3 rounds of user testing.

First-round user testing consisted of face to face interviews (conducted via zoom).

Usability testing as users interacted with a clickable mock prototype of the workspace, and finally a questionnaire consisting of a series of qualitative & quantitative questions.

5. Synthesis

Insights

After first round user testing, key insights included:

  • The need for simplicity
  • An all-in-one location for all inormation regarding webinars
  • Low cognitive load when using

Recommendations derived from these insights included:

  • That the new design be much 'simpler' in appearance and use
  • That this simplicity must extend to 'navigation to' and 'from' the workspace.
  • The importance of design heuristics were noted here.
6. Design

Ideation & Testing

For ideation phase, I used crazy 8's exercise to rapidly develop 8 paper prototypes of prospective designs for the workspace.

For each prototype I focused on altering content layout, orientation, focus on media and wording used. Once created, second round usability testing was then conducted via zoom.

Insights from second round interviews were that:

  • Simplicity was king (again)
  • Any complexity in
    design= confusion
  • Common design heuristics strongly determined the top 2 favourite designs
7. Draft

Ideation

After second-round testing consisting of synthesis and insights, I developed a high fidelity clickable draft prototype of the preferred prototype for third round user testing.

Users reacted positively to the new layout and intuitive design providing further feedback during testing.

It was during this third and final stage that I reached an 'aha moment' where it became clear users were seeking a design layout similar to familiar video streaming platforms already used such as Youtube and Facebook.

This was reaffirmed in a subsequent quantitative questionnaires where users were asked about their preferred method of video streaming consumption.

8. The Solution

Prototype

After three rounds of user testing, a final high fidelity clickable design was created.

The design changes include a much simpler interface (using similar design heuristics found in the most commonly used desktop video streaming platforms today Youtube and Facebook.

Simpler wording along navigation lists, a greater focus on the video player within the workspace, contextual information about headings and videos further improved the user experience significantly.

The overall impression was a joy to use for users and intuitive. A final list of recommendations was also created for the university to review.

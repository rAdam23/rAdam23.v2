RMIT University

UI Design

1. The University

RMIT is a leading Australian university ranked 11th in the world and recognised for its leadership and innovation in technology, arts and design.

2. The Brief

To redesign & improve the user experience of the universities online Canvas platform workspace 'Collaborate ultra'.

3. My Role

User Experience Designer

4. The Final Product

After three rounds of user testing, a final high fidelity clickable design was created.

The design changes include a much simpler interface (using similar design heuristics found in the most commonly used desktop video streaming platforms today Youtube and Facebook.

Simpler wording along navigation lists, a greater focus on the video player within the workspace, contextual information about headings and videos further improved the user experience significantly.

The overall impression was a joy to use for users and intuitive. A final list of recommendations was also created for the university to review.

"Yeah.. I really like this a lot.. its just definitely much easier to use because you know where everything is.." - Emilia, User experience tester

1. The Problem

During my use of RMIT Online's student platform (Canvas)'s online portal 'Collaborate Ultra' it was clear that students had significant difficulty in navigating, using and understanding the workspace. This was confirmed with desk research. My objective was to redesign the UX of the workspace.

2. Lean Personas

Research consisted of developing a lean persona identifying the typical users of the workspace. This lean Persona was developed from the desk research I had conducted through filtering student help requests to see common issues regarding using the platform.

3. User Research & Interviews

Once a lean persona was completed, 9 individuals that matched the lean persona profile were interviewed for 3 rounds of user testing (via zoom due to covid-19 lockdown).

4. The first 2 rounds user testing & affinity mapping & Ideation

First round interviews began with users being provided with figma created clickable prototype of the workspace & interviewed using a series of qualitative & quantitative questions. Each users responses were documented on Miro app and then affinity mapped to identify common themes, responses to each question. Second round testing focused on crazy 8's wireframes developed from key insights.

5. Synthesis of Findings

Over the 2 rounds of user testing, key insights included users preference for simplicity in UI, an 'all-in-one' location for all inormation regarding webinars and low cognitive load when using the space. Heuristic design and user responses demanded the design be familiar to commonly used Youtube and Facebook video platform design.

The recommendations derived from these insights were that the new design be much 'simpler' in appearance and use, that the simplicity must extend to 'navigation to' and 'from' the workspace and the importance of design heuristics were noted here.

6. A Third Round of Testing

After the first 2 rounds of user testing, I developed a clickable high fidelity prototype (using Figma) from the insights of the original platform and responses to crazy 8's wireframes developed during second round interviews.

For this final prototype I focused on eliminating pain points raised during the interviews and incorporating key insights into design.A large video viewing area, contextual titles, content and menus were incorporated.

7. The Final Prototype

After three rounds of user testing, a final high fidelity clickable design was created.

The design changes include a much simpler interface using similar design heuristics to Youtube and Facebook video platforms.

Simpler wording along navigation lists, a greater focus on the video player within the workspace, contextual information about headings and videos further improved the user experience significantly. I also looked to RMIT's logo and branding to ensure the final design felt consistent with the webiste and university branding.

The overall impression was received overwhelmingly positively with users responding overwhelmingly positively to ease of use in this final design. A final list of recommendations from this case study was also created for the university to review.

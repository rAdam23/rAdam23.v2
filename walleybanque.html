1. About

Walleybanque

Walleybanque is a disruptive neobank startup concept based in France. The neobank is the latest player looking to break into the banking industry by utilising its own Ai to assist customers in both saving and keeping track of their funds more easily.

2. My Role

UI Designer

  • Branding
  • Logo Design
  • User Interface Design
  • User Experience Testing
3. The Requirements

The Brief

For this project the bank supplied a brief outlining what they wanted from the design including:

  • Branding
  • Logo Design
  • User Interface Design
  • User Experience Testing

The UX team had also supplied data and desktop wireframes of the current layout from recent user research.

In addition to these, the bank also supplied 2 Lean Personas to assist with the design and ensure all needs and pain points were being addressed.

4. The Users

Needs & Pain Points

For this project the bank supplied a brief outlining what they wanted from the design including:

  • To be able to have a savings tracker to monitor savings rates.
  • To have account balances more discreet in size (current wireframes made users feel uncomfortable their balances were on display).
  • An easy to use tool to help save for 'the little things'.
5. Designing

Moodboards

Inspiration for the project was drawn from a number of sources.

The Ai used by the bank inspired a futuristic direction with the design, while classic 80's cult film Back to The Future 2 was inspiration for the colour gradients.

Blister packets and the latest 2020 trends in Ui design (Neumorphism) were used as the foundation for the design of all cards in the Ui.

Finally, a sportiness from Nike's logo was also inspiration for Walleybanque logo design to keep things simple and modern.

6. Mapping

User Flows

User flows were drafted from existing wireframes and then completely redrafted to better compliment the new design.

The concept of the new design required a simpler user flow to match in order to be successful, so an effort was made to reduce the number of steps users were taking to complete tasks.

This was achieved by removing many of the tabs that were unwanted by users and presenting more information up front and centre as required.

Achieving this enhanced the user experience and elevated the ui design as it presented a solution with both form and function.

7. Draft

Ideation

After second-round testing consisting of synthesis and insights, I developed a high fidelity clickable draft prototype of the preferred prototype for third round user testing.

Users reacted positively to the new layout and intuitive design providing further feedback during testing.

It was during this third and final stage that I reached an 'aha moment' where it became clear users were seeking a design layout similar to familiar video streaming platforms already used such as Youtube and Facebook.

This was reaffirmed in a subsequent quantitative questionnaires where users were asked about their preferred method of video streaming consumption.

8. The Solution

Prototype

From the blister packet design enticing the user into a clean navigation experience, to the sportiness of the Walleybanque's new 'W' logo.

The new design of the app enables users a fesh take on a traditional banking app that is both unique and engaging.

Vibrant brand colouring and contextual simplicity in the card design layouts bring about joy and engagement to users, enabling them to focus on key banking information required of the brief, whilst simultaneously elevating user experience.

Walleybanque

UX UI Design

1. The Organization

Walleybanque is a french neo-bank startup based in Lyon France looking to disrupt the fintech space with its AI-powered savings accounts.

2. The Brief

To create a fully responsive, modern and unique UI for the banks mobile banking platform meeting the requirements set out by the banks UX design team.

3. My Role

User Interface Designer

4. The Final Product

Blister packet inspired buttons with a fresh and contemporary home screen.

The redesign draws inspiration from popular culture brands and movies such as nike's swoosh logo, Marty McFly's iconic rainbow cap in Back to the Future 2 and the latest trends in UI for 2021 including neumorphic design and apple inspired colour gradients.

"Its a really simple layout, its easy to see where everything is and how much I'm saving".. - Wendy, User tester

1. Defining the goal

Walleybanque needed a user interface that assisted both 'savers' and 'spenders' lean personas of their customer base to 'save for the little things' and keep track of savings easily and clearly.

2. Identifying User Needs

The UI needed to be able to limit lean persona 'Spenders' and 'Savers' spending habits, assist with paying bills and monitoring savings. The final design also needed to incorporate Walleybanque's new 'savings buckets' feature and be 'fresh and unique'.

3. Mapping the User flows

I started mapping the existing userflows from the wireframes provided. From here I redesigned the layout of the UI to minimise steps and cognitive load to users.

4. Moodboards

Following a fresh futuristic brief I drew inspiration from iconic brands and pop culture along with the latest neumorphic UI trends.

Colour gradients were inspired from Marty Mcfly's rainbow cap in Back to the future 2, tablet blister packets were inspiration for the neumorphic button shapes, wile Maven Pro font was used for its contemporary sans-serif feel and legibility across devices.

5. Blister Packet Experimentation

I wanted to explore further neumorphism within the final pieces as it was visually clean and providede a better UX, encouraging users to click buttons and interact with the design. So I began playing with proportions.

6. Wireframing Prototypes

While defining userflows and the feel of the project I played around with layouts, experimenting with conventional banking platform layouts. I settled on a card layout design as it fit the brief, clearly defining information in containers, whilst also being exptremely adaptable and responsive to all types of mobile devices today.
